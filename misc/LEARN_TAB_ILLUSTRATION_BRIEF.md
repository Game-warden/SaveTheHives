# Learn Tab Illustration Brief (v2 — corrected)

Reference style: the two watercolor/gouache naturalist meadow scenes Ronnie shared
(bee flying from wildflowers to an old oak with a wild hive in the trunk — warm,
loose brushwork, visible paper texture, soft light, field-guide feel). Every
prompt below should be generated in that same hand, so the whole set reads as
one consistent series.

## What changed from round 1

- **Hero's flight line** was generated as a graceful curved arc at first —
  wrong, since a dead-straight line is the actual content point of Module 1
  (a fed bee flies *straight* home; that's what "beeline" means). Fixed by
  spelling out "ruler-straight, no curve or arc" instead of just "dashed
  line." That phrasing is now baked into prompt #1 below.
- **Border text** ("Nature Journal" / "Field Study Notes" painted vertically
  into the margins) showed up on 2 of the first 5 images but not the other
  3. It's permanent once it's part of a raster image, and the app already
  has its own title/caption text sitting right below each illustration — so
  **decision: drop it everywhere.** Every prompt below now explicitly says
  no border text. (If you'd rather keep it as a consistent motif across all
  five instead, just say so — happy to flip this back.)
- **Terrain chooser** was generated as one composited 2×2 grid image.
  **Decision: split into 4 separate square files instead** (open field,
  river, hilltop, woods), each its own image with just its own badge — this
  lets the app lay them out in its own grid with the correct corner radius
  and spacing instead of inheriting whatever grid lines/margins are baked
  into a single composite. Prompt #3 below is now four prompts, 3a–3d.

## Shared style guide — paste into every prompt

> Medium: naturalist watercolor and gouache field-guide illustration, loose
> confident brushwork with visible paper texture, soft edges, warm and
> slightly muted palette (sage green, dusty lavender, buttery yellow, warm
> cream, soft sky blue). Not flat, not vector, not cartoon — painterly like a
> vintage nature journal.
>
> Consistent bee character across the whole set: a fuzzy honeybee with a
> black-and-amber banded abdomen and translucent veined wings, rendered in
> the same fine ink-and-wash linework each time.
>
> No text, no lettering, no captions, no journal-style border text of any
> kind anywhere in the image — a completely clean edge on all four sides.
> The only exception is a small circular badge with a single letter, and
> only where a prompt explicitly asks for one.

## 1. Hero — "The Beeline" (Module 1 + hub landing) — ALREADY APPROVED

A sunlit wildflower meadow at the edge of a hedgerow. In the foreground:
lavender, purple asters, and yellow buttercups, with a single honeybee
lifting off one bloom. **A single ruler-straight dashed flight line — no
curve, no arc, perfectly straight** — runs from the bee across the meadow to
a large, weathered oak in the middle distance, where a wild colony's comb is
visible inside a hollow in the trunk, with a small stream of bees entering
and leaving. Rolling countryside, a wooden rail fence, soft hills behind.
Warm late-afternoon light. No border text.

**Aspect ratio:** ~2.2:1 landscape (the approved version's ratio — keep it).
**Export:** 2x for retina, no smaller than 2000px on the long edge.

## 2. Triangulation — "Two Lines, One Answer" (Module 3) — ALREADY APPROVED

Same style and palette as #1. A wide meadow scene with two small painted
stake-markers, one labeled **A** and one labeled **B**, spaced well apart,
each trailing a straight dashed sightline in warm honey-gold ink wash — the
two lines converging at a single point: a large old oak with a wild hive
opening in its trunk. A honeybee rests on a flower near the base of each
stake. Rolling hills and a wooden rail fence in the background. No border
text.

**Aspect ratio:** ~2.2:1 landscape, matching #1. **Export:** 2x for retina.

## 3a. Terrain Chooser — Open Field (Module 4, panel A)

A single square watercolor vignette, same palette/style as #1: an open,
sunlit hayfield with a few distant hay bales, a single honeybee in the
foreground, and one straight dashed line running across the open ground
toward the horizon. Warm golden light. In the top-right corner, a small
hand-painted circular badge with the letter **A** in warm brown ink. No
other text.

**Aspect ratio:** 1:1. **Export:** ~1000×1000px, 2x.

## 3b. Terrain Chooser — Blocked by River (Module 4, panel B)

Same square format and style as 3a: a landscape split by a winding blue
river, a honeybee in the foreground, and a dashed line that runs alongside
the riverbank rather than straight across it — visually showing the path
rerouted around the water. Small circular badge, letter **B**, top-right
corner. No other text.

**Aspect ratio:** 1:1. **Export:** ~1000×1000px, 2x.

## 3c. Terrain Chooser — Single Vantage / Hilltop (Module 4, panel C)

Same square format and style: a low grassy hill, a single small human
figure standing at the top looking out over the land, a honeybee flying
above. Open sky, soft distant hills beyond. Small circular badge, letter
**C**, top-right corner. No other text.

**Aspect ratio:** 1:1. **Export:** ~1000×1000px, 2x.

## 3d. Terrain Chooser — Dense Woods (Module 4, panel B→A)

Same square format and style: a dense, shadowy stand of trees, dappled
light filtering through the canopy, one or two honeybees visible among the
undergrowth, a faint broken dashed path weaving between the trunks. Small
circular badge reading **"B→A"** in the bottom-right corner. No other text.

**Aspect ratio:** 1:1, matching 3a–3c exactly (same crop/zoom level so the
four sit together as a clean set). **Export:** ~1000×1000px, 2x.

## 4. Bee Box & Kit — flat-lay (Module 6) — ALREADY APPROVED

A flat-lay watercolor, viewed from above on a weathered wooden table.
Center: an open two-compartment wooden bee-lining box, lid slid back, one
empty catch compartment and one feed compartment with a small piece of
honeycomb glistening with syrup, a bee resting on it. Arranged around it
like a specimen page: a small brass baseplate compass (open, red needle), a
marking pen with a small dab of white paint beside it, a small corked glass
jar of syrup, and a pocket stopwatch. Soft natural shadows, slightly
worn/vintage object rendering. No text.

**Aspect ratio:** ~1.7:1 landscape (the approved version's ratio — keep it).
**Export:** 2x for retina.

## 5. Waggle Decoder — inside the hive (Module 9) — ALREADY APPROVED

A more intimate, darker-toned watercolor set inside a dim wild hive — a
single honeybee mid-waggle-dance on the vertical face of a comb, body
angled diagonally, a soft shimmer along her path suggesting the "waggle
run." A faint warm sunbeam glyph glows in the upper corner with a thin
dashed line running straight down to the top of the comb, and a **second
dashed line running at the same angle as the bee's own path** — the two
lines parallel to each other, visually linking the sun's direction to the
dance's angle. Cozy golden interior light against warm dark comb tones. No
text.

**Aspect ratio:** ~4:3, matching the approved version. **Export:** 2x for
retina.

## Integration notes for whoever wires these into the app

- Current build uses inline SVG driven by CSS variables, which is why it
  automatically restyles for light/dark mode. Static raster art (PNG/JPG)
  won't do that automatically — decide whether to: (a) use the same image
  in both themes (simplest, and honestly fine since these are warm/cream by
  design), or (b) commission a second, darker-background pass for dark mode.
- Reader/hub container is capped at `max-width: 560px` — export at 2x the
  target display size for retina sharpness within that column.
- Terrain chooser ships as 4 separate files (3a–3d), not one composited
  grid — the app will lay them out in its own 2×2 CSS grid.
- Replace each `lvDiagramSVG(id)` case in `app.js` with an `<img>` tag
  pointing at the exported file(s) once art is in hand; keep the same
  `figcaption` copy already written per module.
