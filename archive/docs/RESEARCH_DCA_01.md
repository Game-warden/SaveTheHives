# Topographic Prediction of Drone Congregation Areas (DCAs)

Project: SaveTheHives_2
Date: 2026-02-26

## 1. The "Bowl" Effect & Topographic Aspect
Research confirms that DCAs are not randomly placed but are significantly influenced by the Aspect (slope direction) and local topography to provide wind shelter and thermal stability.

* **Slope & Aspect**: GIS landscape analysis identifies slope direction as a primary predictor, showing a distinct preference for south-facing aspects to maximize solar gain.
* **Wind Sheltering**: DCAs are frequently located in "depressed" open areas or pastures surrounded by high vegetation, creating a sheltered "bowl" that concentrates pheromone trails.

**Reference**: Galindo-Cardona et al. (2012).

## 2. Visual Landmarks & Horizon Contrast
Drones utilize prominent geographic features for navigation and multi-year site fidelity.

* **Leading Lines**: Radar tracking indicates drones follow "flyways" along forest edges, tree lines, and trail intersections.
* **Horizon Re-orientation**: Flight paths are often re-oriented based on visual "notches" or dips in the tree line appearing on the 200–250m horizon.

**Reference**: Woodgate et al. (2021).

## 3. Thermal Updrafts & Compass Orientation
The Physical DCA Hypothesis posits that static topographic features create microclimates attractive to drones.

* **Static Locations**: Topography-based DCAs can persist at the same coordinates for decades, regardless of colony turnover.
* **Learning Flights**: Drones perform specific orientation flights to memorize ridges and valleys used to locate these hubs.

**Reference**: Koeniger & Koeniger (2014).
